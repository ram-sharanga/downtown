Investor Presentation

1. Industry Outlook: The Current Ticketing Market

The global online ticketing market was valued at approximately $60 billion in 2023, with the Indian market projected to reach $2.67 billion by 2023 and growing at a CAGR of 8.6% until 2030. The rise of digital platforms has led to increased demand for event and ticket booking services, especially for entertainment, sports, and travel.

However, there are several disadvantages and challenges that the current ticketing platforms face:

  • High Transaction Fees: Traditional ticketing platforms typically charge high service fees, which can range from 10% to 30% of the ticket price, making it expensive for both event organizers and consumers.
  • Limited Vendor Access: Platforms often rely on centralized systems, leading to limited visibility and access for smaller or local vendors and event organizers, who have to go through multiple intermediaries to get their events listed.
  • Marketing and Customer Acquisition Costs: Ticketing platforms spend a large portion of their budgets on user acquisition, often through expensive ads and promotions. Customer acquisition costs (CAC) can account for 15-20% of revenue.
  • Fraud and Security Risks: Centralized platforms are vulnerable to fraud, including fake ticket sales, identity theft, and payment fraud, leading to an increase in operational costs and a loss of customer trust.

2. ONDC: A Game-Changing Opportunity

ONDC (Open Network for Digital Commerce) is an open-source, decentralized network designed to democratize digital commerce and reduce dependency on centralized platforms. ONDC provides a standardized framework for buyer and seller apps to interact, offering a more open and transparent system.

Why Building on ONDC is a Game Changer:

  • Reduced Intermediaries: ONDC reduces the need for intermediaries and aggregators, thus lowering transaction costs.
  • Wider Vendor Access: With ONDC, event organizers can list their events across multiple buyer apps, increasing visibility and access without paying for expensive platform listings.
  • Decentralized Marketing: ONDC enables a decentralized network, allowing marketing efforts to be distributed across multiple apps, reducing the cost burden on any single platform.
  • Improved Security: By adopting ONDC's decentralized framework and potentially incorporating blockchain, the platform can minimize fraud and enhance transaction security.
  • Scalability: ONDC offers a robust infrastructure that enables scalable growth without the need for major upfront investments in physical infrastructure.

3. How We Intend to Solve Current Industry Disadvantages with ONDC

a. Lower Transaction Fees:

Current Problem: Traditional ticketing platforms charge high service fees, which can eat into event organizers’ revenue and increase ticket prices for consumers.

Our Solution: ONDC’s architecture allows us to connect vendors directly with multiple buyer apps, eliminating intermediaries. This drastically reduces the fees charged by traditional platforms and payment gateways, enabling us to offer lower transaction fees while maintaining profitability. By integrating with multiple payment gateways, we can leverage bulk discounts and offer a reduction in payment processing costs by 20-30%.

b. Democratizing Access to Vendors:

Current Problem: Smaller event organizers often struggle to gain visibility on large, centralized ticketing platforms. The reliance on a single platform limits their ability to reach a broad audience.

Our Solution: ONDC’s open ecosystem allows event organizers to list their events on multiple buyer apps simultaneously. This increases access to a wider audience without requiring significant marketing investments from the organizers. This lowers the customer acquisition cost for both vendors and our platform by sharing marketing efforts across multiple apps.

c. Cost Reduction through Shared Marketing:

Current Problem: Traditional platforms spend a large portion of their revenue on expensive digital ads and user acquisition strategies. This significantly impacts profit margins.

Our Solution: With ONDC, we benefit from the network effect, where multiple buyer apps collectively drive traffic to event listings. The marketing and customer acquisition costs are shared across the ecosystem, enabling us to lower the customer acquisition cost from 15-20% to 5-10%. This allows for more efficient marketing spend and a stronger ROI.

d. Enhanced Security and Fraud Prevention:

Current Problem: Fraud and security risks such as ticket duplication, identity theft, and payment fraud are prevalent on centralized platforms, leading to higher operational costs and potential loss of consumer trust.

Our Solution: ONDC’s decentralized nature, combined with blockchain or distributed ledger technology (DLT), provides greater security. We can reduce fraud risks and ensure a higher level of transaction integrity. Blockchain integration could potentially lower fraud-related costs by 15-25% and improve overall platform security.

e. Scalability and Operational Efficiency:

Current Problem: Traditional ticketing platforms face challenges when it comes to scaling, requiring substantial investments in infrastructure, servers, and human resources.

Our Solution: ONDC allows us to scale efficiently without investing heavily in physical infrastructure. As the network grows, we can leverage ONDC’s existing framework, ensuring cost-efficient scaling without the need for major upfront investments. This provides long-term sustainability and low operational overheads.

f. Automated Operations and AI Integration:

Current Problem: Managing event listings, ticket availability, and pricing manually is time-consuming and prone to errors.

Our Solution: We will leverage AI-driven automation for demand forecasting, dynamic pricing, and real-time inventory updates. Automation will also streamline customer service operations using AI chatbots, reducing the need for large customer support teams and significantly lowering operational costs.

Conclusion

By building our ticketing platform on ONDC, we are positioned to disrupt the current industry’s status quo. We will reduce transaction fees, democratize vendor access, lower customer acquisition costs, enhance security, and provide scalability—all while maintaining profitability. ONDC’s open, decentralized framework not only offers a more efficient and cost-effective way to run a ticketing platform but also ensures that both vendors and consumers benefit from a more transparent and fair system. This unique approach allows us to offer a compelling competitive advantage in the rapidly growing online ticketing market.
